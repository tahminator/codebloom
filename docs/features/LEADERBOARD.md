The leaderboard pages are one of the most important features to Codebloom, as it directly allows users to compete within:

- the global leaderboard against all users
- any university leaderbaord they are a part of against all students in the university
- any clubs they are a part of against all members of the club

and any combination of them for that matter - you can mix and match filters to compare user performances. You can even turn on one or many filters & enable `Toggle Global Index`:

<img src="/screenshots/toggle-global-rank.png" alt="Toggle Global Rank with Cornell and Columbia filter enabled" >

which will show you the same users, but their ranks will now indicate how they are doing across ALL users in Codebloom.

The leaderboard pages are fully paginated, with custom filters & search queries.

> [!NOTE]

## `/leaderboard` - Current leaderboard

This page will always show the currently active leaderboard.

[Goto `codebloom.patinanetwork.org/leaderboard`](https://codebloom.patinanetwork.org/leaderboard)

## `/leaderboard/all` - List of all leaderboards

This page will show the list of all leaderboards. You can click on a previous leaderboard to view the leaderboard exactly as that leaderboard ended.

[Goto `codebloom.patinanetwork.org/leaderboard/all`](https://codebloom.patinanetwork.org/leaderboard/all)

## `/leaderboard/{leaderboardId}` - View specific ID

This page will show the leaderboard which is connected to `{leaderboardId}`. This is in full feature parity with `/leaderboard`.

[Goto `codebloom.patinanetwork.org/leaderboard/dcf49998-604b-4240-8711-4456c49d79e7`](https://codebloom.patinanetwork.org/leaderboard/dcf49998-604b-4240-8711-4456c49d79e7), the very first Codebloom leaderboard named `Beta Launch Leaderboard`
